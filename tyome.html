Case studies / Tyome

When

July 2022

About

Tyome is a comprehensive IoT - cloud solution that encompasses both software and hardware solutions.

Scope

This project is dedicated to companies with meeting spaces, optimises the management and reservation process, thus aiding in the rapid digitisation of the entire process.

The system is quite complex, having functionalities ranging from room statistics to office map & guidance, maintaining rooms booking up to date.

What I’ve done

Working on this project my role was to conduct competition research to identify trends and design differentiators and to developed user flows, wireframes, low and high-fidelity prototypes, and conducted systematic design and testing procedures.

Challenge

Tyome could be ensuring seamless integration with existing workplace technologies. As organizations may already have various software solutions and tools in place, the challenge lies in creating a smooth and efficient integration process.

Design Process

My journey began with a thorough discovery process, where I uncovered the various challenges and limitations of the current reservation process. With this valuable insight, I set out to create a more efficient & user-friendly application that can significantly shortens the time required for this action.

Competitors

During research process, I examined competitor and similar platforms, analysing their UX, UI, and user flows, with a specific focus on the reservation process.

Research

To better understand user needs, goals, and behaviour, we used several methods to extrapolate information relevant to the product, but also to better understand the needs of users.

Data Analysis

I gathered insights from our audience and analysed the data to identify common insights, pain points & goals. This helped me define the needs for what the application should address.

Solution

I came up with the solution to have tablets in front of each room with the application & a desktop application. The application should be customised according to the role that someone has in the company.

The main challenge was to design a application that enable users to book a room with the help of recommended actions, real-time data and room status.

And finally explored information architecture. It involves organising and structuring information to enhance usability. By designing the information architecture effectively, we can improve user experience and make information easier to navigate and understand.

Tablet App

Presented here are a series of screens from the app, intentionally avoiding complete flows to preserve the confidentiality of certain app features. My focus here is to provide a glimpse into the design without revealing all aspects of the app's functionality.

Results

The solution have been well-received when presented. Tyome received positive feedback from our management, stakeholders and colleagues.

The solution was not implemented yet in the company, but we took the chance to explore different paths to take, experiment with ideas and gather feedback for the proposed solution.
